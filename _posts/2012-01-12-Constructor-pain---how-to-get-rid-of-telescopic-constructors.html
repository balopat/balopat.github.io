Unfortunately I had a lot of chance to meet Telescopic Constructors in my carrier and accepted it as a necessary evil. Now I found an antidote.





Yes, These constructors can become very nasty, because they are named the same whereas in fact they are representing the creation of different cases.

A loan calculator is a good example for this where the different type of loan-products - as they are based on a single algorithm - are differ only in parameter values. I have seen real life loan calculator constructors marked with stray, eventually outdated comments about which is good for what.

I recently ran into a simple and alternative solution what I read in Joshua Keriewsky's book, which I highly recommend to every software developer: Refactoring to Patterns

The solution is to use creation methods instead of the constructors:
http://industriallogic.com/xp/refactoring/constructorCreation.html

The only drawback is that if you start this refactoring, the creation of your objects will be inconsistent throughout the system.

The huge benefit is the intention revealing code.
